Subtractem’s Fire Trap Leveling Guide

Before we start: this guide is intended to be as beginner friendly as possible. In many cases I chose simpler routes or skills that I feel appeal to a broader audience. I am aware of many common cases or setups that would be faster and, in some cases, even easier to play but that may turn off swaths of players who need guides.

The general idea here is that we are going to level from Act 1 until right before the end of Act 3 with Rolling Magma/Blazing Salvo + Holy Flame Totem + Flame Wall (adding Armageddon Brand at level 28).

Also, you’ll note that this guide is 99% about Acts 1-3. This is because the vast majority of what matters specifically to leveling this build all happens at the beginning. After Act 4 we have access to all gems and the rest of the experience is very rote.

The Havoc leveling showcase below is an entire run that you can use to follow in more detail but we aren’t going to focus on Acts 4-10.

Let’s Begin.

Act 1

Prep

  • Set up Tytykiller’s AAAACaster leveling filter. Tyty has the best filters for leveling, they are the gold standard for speedrunning. While they aren’t perfect for Softcore Trade economy goals they make leveling so much smoother and enjoyable I recommend them without hesitation. These are this filters: https://bit.ly/tyFilter
  • You will then need to download the sounds from his discord in the #filter channel here: https://discord.gg/bxZ9cAuz4v and put them in your Documents/My Games/Path of Exile folder
  • I will provide filters to change to after you kill Kitava after Filterblade updates

Recurring Goals

  • From vendor: get Movement Speed boots/Blue-Blue-Blue] linked items/+1 fire level scepters/wands
  • Use this search string every time you’re in town on Tarkleigh/Nessa: "level.*fire|nne|b-b-b|r-b|b-r"
  • Look out for Red - Blue linked items for Holy Flame Totem and Steelskin later
  • Make Sapphire Rings for cold resist for Merveil (can also use in above recipe)
  • Vendor recipe: Iron Ring + blue gem
  • Pick up all blue/rare items and sell them (usually unidentified) for Transmute shards, sell Transmutes/Blacksmith’s/Armourers for Wisdom Scrolls to identify items that you can sell for Alteration shards when needed
  • Keep your Arcane Surge at level 1-2 (right click the plus sign so it doesn’t show up any more to level). This is so it doesn’t require as much mana to be active.
  • I will mention wand/scepter in Act 1 but we STRONGLY prefer Scepters - early on Wands are better now after the flat damage recipe removal but Scepters are still better later. Also, later on we will use Leap Slam for mobility which requires scepters.

Steps

  1. Kill Hillock on The Beach, take everything he drops. Identify Boots for movement speed, use any wands/scepters he drops.
  2. Talk to Nessa and Tarkleigh, sell everything you’re not using. Use the Vendor Search String to look for the items listed above.
  3. Take Rolling Magma [RM] from Tarkleigh. RM isn’t the most comfortable skill but it’s a LOT of damage. You want to aim it right in front of the feet of the enemy, it’ll bounce and can hit multiple times. If you really hate RM you can continue to just use Fireball or even Freezing Pulse instead.
  4. Kill Hailrake at Tidal Island. Go to the Coast and find the Waypoint, from there take The Tidal Island exit.

Circumnavigate The Tidal Island where you will find Hailrake near a shipwreck. He has a very annoying Glacial Cascade attack that he will aim at where you were standing (or walking to!) when he casts it. Stand mid-range from him and attack at a distance. Wait until the last second when the spell comes out of his hands and then change direction, done properly he will never hit you.

Hailrake will ALWAYS drop a Portal Scroll. Grab the Medicine Chest and all of his loot and then Exit to Character Screen (can use a logout macro) to save using the Portal Scroll.

Talk to Nessa, take Quicksilver Flask and Elemental Proliferation for your quest reward.

Link Rolling Magma + Elemental Proliferation (and arcane surge if you have a 3 blue link).

  1. The Mud Flats

This zone, and the Rhoas in it, are often joked about as being the hardest thing in the game until uber bosses. There is a bit of truth to this. They will charge you from off screen, chain stun you, and kill you. The best recommendation is to change your direction perpendicular to where you were walking fairly often and take it slow so you don’t get overwhelmed. Rolling Magma can do a lot of work here clearing monsters all around the screen.

The goal here is to get three Glyphs from the Rhoa Nests around The Mud Flats. These nests are connected by dry riverbeds that you can follow to guide you to the next one. After gathering all 3, you can place them on the Glyph wall that will open the entrance to the Submerged Passage.

  1. Add Holy Flame Totem, Flame Wall, Frostblink, and Steelskin

Take the Waypoint back to town and talk to Tarkleigh. Take Flame Wall and Frostblink from him. Remember to use the vendor search string for movement speed boots and B-B-B links if you don’t have them yet.

Talk to Nessa and buy Holy Flame Totem and Steelskin from her.

Buy a heavy belt + jade amulet off of her to fill out stats that you’re likely missing.

The playstyle here is that we cast Flame Wall on or in front of the enemy, place Holy Flame Totem behind it, and cast Rolling Magma through it. All projectiles passing through Flame Wall get bonus Fire damage so this acts as a huge damage boost for our other skills. Put Steelskin on your left click.

  1. The Submerged Passage + The Flooded Depths / The Ledge / The Climb

This speedrunning trick is super easy and saves a bit of time. What you’re going to do is look for The Flooded Depths entrance. It’s a cave in the ground.

Place a portal near it and then continue on to find the entrance to The Ledge.

Take The Ledge waypoint back to Lioneye’s Watch and then take the portal from town, this will bring you back to The Flooded Depths. In here you’ll run to the end to find a big crab.

Since we navigated to The Ledge our character likely leveled up 1-2 more times and this crab boss should feel super easy. Exit to character selection and log back in to go to town and get your skill point book.

Run through The Ledge (straight line)/The Climb, kill Kuduku/Skeleton/Goat bosses if you need Transmutes/Alterations, then continue to The Prison.

  1. The Prison

Do the lab trial in The Lower Prison, then continue on through The Upper Prison and then to The Warden’s Quarters. You can level 1-2 more times here if you’re not comfortable with fighting Brutus yet. The build should be feeling very strong at this point.

  1. Brutus

The easiest thing for Brutus is just make sure you have plenty of life. Around 200-250 you should be fairly comfortable. Use 3 life flasks if you want to be very careful. You can buy Coral Rings/Leather Belt from Nessa for an easy life bump as well.

You can cheese Brutus by placing your Holy Flame Totem + Flame Wall in a doorway or at the edge of a pillar and staying on the other side of it. Brutus’ “come” pull move will hit your totem instead of you.

Here is an example clip: https://clips.twitch.tv/PrettiestSuspiciousMochaFeelsBadMan-e9Tp08nQwzhgqZN1

  1. Post-Brutus & The Ship Graveyard

After killing Brutus, take the Prisoner’s Gate waypoint back to town. Grab Clarity off of Tarkleigh and buy Vitality off of Nessa. Use them.

Link Rolling Magma + Combustion + Elemental Proliferation (or Added Lightning Damage if you prefer more single target, but this is more transmute pressure)

If you have a friend or are in a guild, this is when Leap Slam becomes available for many classes. Bribe a friend to give you a Leap Slam gem, if you’re using Scepters you can use it now and leveling becomes much, MUCH faster and more fun.

Watch this Tytykiller video to learn about using Leap Slam + Frost Blink. If you’re in SSF or have no friends you will have to wait until you do The Library in Act 3. https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Su-VWnovUlM

Continue on from The Prisoner’s Gate to The Ship Graveyard. Grab the Waypoint first and then you’re going to be looking for a caved-in Shipwreck.

Go into the cave and find a Slave Girl, click on her to get the Allflame. Return to The Ship Graveyard and find Captain Fairgraves on a raised platform. Give him the Allflame and it will start a small fight. Go back to Bestel in town for a Skill point.

  1.  Merveil and the end of Act 1

From here it’ll be a fairly straight shot to the end of Act 1.

You’ll continue on from The Ship Graveyard to The Cavern of Wrath and then the Cavern of Anger into the Merveil fight.

Consider trying Blazing Salvo here, it’s less damage than Rolling Magma but you might find it more comfortable to aim. It is available from Nessa.

Before fighting Merveil, aim to have 60%+ Cold Resistance. The closer to 75% cold resistance the better. Also aim to have about 300 life (general rule of thumb is 300 life per act to be comfortable).

The Merveil fight is fairly straightforward. Phase 1 she’s in human-ish form, casting a blizzard spell and doing cold attacks. With high cold res you should be nearly immortal unless you stand in a bunch of abilities at once.

After depleting her health bar she will transform into a Sea Witch and summon lots of adds. Rolling Magma/Blazing Salvo and Holy Flame Totem should make all of this easy work, just don’t stand in the water geysers.

Act 2

This Act has very few structural changes to the build. We aren’t going to do any vendor recipes and we only add 3 utility skills to the build: Herald of Ash, Wave of Conviction, and Skitterbots.

  1. Rush Chamber of Sins. Go right out of the Forest Encampment to The Old Fields.
  1. Optional is doing The Den quest, find this cave entrance and kill the big white bear inside, you can get a second quicksilver flask (if you don’t have yet) or a belt upgrade from Yeena.

Follow the road through The Old Fields to The Crossroads and find the waypoint along the road. Take a left turn at the crossroads to find the entrance to The Chamber of Sins.

Go down to the second floor, do the Lab trial, and then find and kill Fidelitas at the end of a very long L shaped hallway.

Take the Balefire gem and return to town. Take Herald of Ash from Gruest. Talk to Silk and Yeena and buy Skitterbots and Wave of Conviction from her.

Once you have Deep Thoughts and the mana mastery for 12% reservation efficiency you should be able to run most of your auras, you may have to drop Vitality or Clarity for a while (depending on the gear that you find).

Wave of Conviction gives us exposure for -15 res on enemies, use this to debuff harder monsters/bosses before doing damage.

There are no more changes to the build in Act 2.

  1. Go back to the crossroads, go down to the right to the Felshrine Ruins to do your Lab Trial.
  2. Take the waypoint back to the crossroads and go up to the right to The Broken Bridge and kill Kraityn. He will be across a bridge on the right side of the map.

  1. Go down to the left from town to The Riverways.
  2. Go to The Wetlands and kill Oak.
  3. Go to The Western Forest and kill Alira. You can find her by following the cobblestone path off of the road that is denoted by a torch. She is on the same side of the road as the waypoint.

  1. Follow the road to the end and kill The Blackguards, use the Thaumetic Emblem on the wall. Return to act 1 to get a skill point.
  2. Take the waypoint back to The Western Forest and find The Weaver’s Chambers and kill The Weaver. The Chambers will be on the opposite side of the zone as Alira and denoted by spiderwebs.

  1. Return to Eramir to complete the quest.
  2.  Take the Wetlands waypoint and clear the blockage to enter The Vaal Ruins.
  3.  Continue, skipping The Dread Thicket, to The Caverns. Kill The Vaal Oversoul at the end and complete Act 2.

Act 3

Walk up and to the left to find Clarissa tied up on a post, kill the guards and free her. Make sure that you talk to her before leaving, otherwise you will have a long sad walk back.

Go left along the water’s edge until you find the entrance to The Sarn Encampment.

Follow the path and directly enter The Slums, you don’t have to talk to anyone in town.

  • Enter the Crematorium, do the Lab Trial, find Tolman, get his bracelet and return to town.
  • Talk to Clarissa, she will give you keys to The Sewers.
  • Take Flammability as a quest reward
  • Go back to The Slums and find the sewer grate to enter The Sewers
  • Find Three Platinum Busts for a skill point quest back in town
  • Exit The Sewers into The Marketplace
  • Find the Waypoint, nearby will be The Catacombs, do the lab trial in there
  • From The Marketplace Waypoint continue to The Battlefront

From the entrance to The Battlefront, go to the left. You will find the waypoint and the Blackguard Chest, take the ribbon spool from it.

In the upper left corner of The Battlefront you will find The Docks, explore the docks and find a supply container with Thaumetic Sulphite, take this also.

Go back to The Battlefront and find the entrance to The Solaris Temple. Navigate down to the second floor and then find Dialla at the end of it. She will take your items and exchange them for the Infernal Talc.

Return to The Sewers, you will now be able to clear the blockage to the left of the waypoint. Enter The Ebony Barracks.

Go up and to the left, you will find Gravicius. He can be a bit of a pain to fight if you’re underleveled. Stay out of his Firestorm and watch out for his Molten Shell exploding as well. Take your time and use the terrain to your advantage. Holy Flame Totem can do work here.

Return to town, talk to Maramoa and then buy Armageddon Brand off of Clarissa. Replace Rolling Magma/Blazing Salvo with it. It will do a bit less damage but since it continually casts while you can run around it is very comfortable to use.

Return to The Ebony Barracks and go to the right to enter The Imperial Gardens. Straight up from the waypoint you will find the final lab trial before you ascend to Elementalist. I recommend also grabbing the waypoint in the The Library as well, up and to the left in this area.

If you are lower than level 28, I recommend doing the Siosa quest in The Library first, killing more monsters than usual to get some XP. You have to find a Loose Candle to go to the second floor, then pick up the Golden Pages and return them to Siosa on the first floor.

He will give you a Burning Damage support as a quest reward, you can also buy Leap Slam + Faster Attacks from him.

You can do lab first here if you prefer but I recommend being at least level 28 since lab is level 33.

Go back to town and enter the Aspirant’s Plaza from the green statue at the top of the stairs. It will tell you which labs you missed if you missed any.

Lab can be a pain. Take your time and let Armageddon Brand do most of the work. Keep circling Izaro and don’t stop moving.

I recommend PoE Lab for layout tips, normal lab is usually a straight line so this won’t be important until later: https://www.poelab.com/

After beating Izaro, choose Elementalist and take Shaper of Flames. This is a HUGE power spike and the rest of the leveling process is much, much easier. Now we add in Fire Trap.

  • Buy Fire Trap in Act 1
  • Trap & Mine Damage in Act 2

If you like, you can still use Armageddon Brand + Combustion + Burning Damage for a little additional damage/easier clear until Frostblink entirely gets online but that is a personal choice. It is not necessary!

It’s at this point that I recommend dropping Holy Flame Totem and Flame Wall. Salute your hardworking gems that carried you through the hardest part of the Acts and soldier on.

As you acquire more links in your gear you can add in (depending on socket colors) any of these to both Fire Trap and Frostblink:

  • Efficacy
  • Swift Affliction
  • Deadly Ailments (less good on Fire Trap because it doesn’t help the burning ground, but is ok)
  • Lifetap (great any time mana feels uncomfortable)
  • Ignite Proliferation (get on Frostblink ASAP in Act 4)

Return to The Ebony Barracks and go up to find the Lunaris Temple. At the end of the lower level of it you will fight Piety.

Since we chose to fight her later than a regular run does (usually she is fought before lab) she should feel very easy.

Grab the bench craft recipe from her arena and go to your hideout to the Crafting Bench.

If you have weak weapons with an open prefix and no flat damage to spells, you can craft flat cold (fire is level 40 for some reason) at level 24+, this should be a really nice bump in damage if you need an upgrade. You will want to do this before Dominus.

I also recommend crafting life/resists on your gear as available. Aim for 900-ish life and at near capped fire/lightning resists for the Dominus fight.

Talk to the NPCs in town to complete any remaining quests to get the key to The Sceptre of God.

Return to The Imperial Gardens and enter The Sceptre of God. Navigate to the top and fight Dominus.

You will fight a bunch of monsters before Dominus comes out. Armageddon Brand and Fire Trap should just obliterate them.

Dominus shouldn’t really hurt you too much but watch out for his slam (THE TOUCH OF GOD).

When he goes into his final phase, just stay close to him and your skills should kill him quickly.

Continue to Act 4

Other little tidbits:

In Act 4 we can add Ignite Proliferation to Frostblink and the build is essentially done from a Gem perspective at this point. Keep following the tree and converging toward the final setup.

In Act 5 I recommend working toward using Determination and using a Granite Flask, this helps a lot for smoothing out the spike in physical damage.

After killing Kitava in Act 5 get your resists up (especially Fire and Cold) before venturing out in Act 6.

And from there the build is pretty much done from a gem/skill progression standpoint. Follow along with Havoc in the playthrough for step by step demonstration of what you need to do in Acts 5-10 but the build is effectively done until gear enables us to change/add other skills!

I hope you have a great time leveling and playing this build and feel free to leave some feedback on things I could clarify to make this better. Cheers!
